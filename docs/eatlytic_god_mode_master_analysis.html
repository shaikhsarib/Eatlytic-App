PHYSICS
Atwater Physics Gate
Thermodynamic truth verification — laws of physics don't change
What it is
Calorie math (protein×4 + carbs×4 + fat×9), macro hierarchy guard, gross mass cap (≤103g per 100g). Per-category tolerances (snack 25%, condiment 30%). NOVA-4 + Lie Detector on top.
Why it can't be replicated by scale
This is deterministic code, not ML. Throwing GPUs at it doesn't help. It runs in microseconds. The physics is absolute — nobody can train a model to "do it better." They'd have to write the same equations.
Real moat signal
Current bug: zero-calorie bypass (BUG-01). Fix this first. Once fixed, Eatlytic becomes the only app in India that can provably catch fraudulent food labels using physics — that's a media story, a regulatory angle, and a clinical trust builder.
Who this kills: Yuka (no math)HealthifyMe (no physics)MyFitnessPal (no gate)
DOMAIN DATA
Indian Additive Intelligence + FSSAI Lexicon
Years of manual curation — Maida (GI 110-185), INS codes, FSSAI alerts
What it is
INDIAN_INGREDIENT_LEXICON in brain.py, 67-entry additives.json (needs expansion to 500), INS/E-number O(1) lookup, FSSAI regulatory status, glycemic mappings for Indian-specific starches.
Why Google can't replicate it
Google has no business incentive to build India-specific FSSAI additive intelligence. This requires food chemists who know Indian products, FSSAI regulation tracking, and years of manual verification. No amount of Gemini calls produces this.
The 433-entry gap
additives.json claims 500 entries, has 67. Maltodextrin isn't even in it — it's in brain.py's lexicon instead. This gap is a 4-week sprint, not a 4-year problem. The data exists; it just needs migrating and expanding.
Compounds over time: every admin verification makes the next scan free and instant
CLINICAL TRUST
Dietitian Cohort + Genomic Personalization
Once clinicians plug in patient cohorts, Eatlytic becomes medical infrastructure
What it is
TCF7L2 (T2D risk), AGT (hypertension), LCT (lactose) SNP overlays. HbA1c + fasting glucose biomarker gates. Dietitian cohort portal. Anonymous MD5 patient IDs. Score overrides based on genomic profile.
Switching cost lock-in
Once a dietitian connects 50 patients, their scan history and genomic profiles live in Eatlytic. Moving to a competitor means losing all historical data + retraining patients on a new app. YC's Alex Immerman: "the collaboration layer becomes the moat."
The multiplayer unlock
Patient scans → dietitian sees aggregate excursions → adjusts meal plan → patient rescans → loop tightens. This multi-agent collaboration creates data no competitor has. This is the "Vertical AI Multiplayer" thesis from a16z in practice.
YC explicitly funds this: Ankit Gupta (YC) — "AI Personalized Medicine using genome scan + EHR data"
PRIVACY ARCH
DPDP-Compliant Stateless Genomics
Cookie device_key architecture — clinical personalization with zero PII collection
What it is
Genomic profiles linked to device_key cookies only. No email, no name collected. DPDP erasure via /api/v1/user/delete. SNP overlays computed locally against stored profiles. Stateless by design.
Why big companies fear this space
Google and Meta building genomic health features in India faces DPDP regulatory exposure worth billions. Eatlytic's stateless architecture is a legal shield. Big companies will eventually require account creation, email, and consent flows — Eatlytic's friction-free model wins on UX.
a16z alignment
"Secrets-as-a-service" (Adeniyi Abiodun): "healthcare requires companies to keep sensitive data private." Eatlytic's cookie-based genomic architecture is exactly this — private by architecture, not by policy promise.
COMPOUND CACHE
pHash Verified Product DB + Admin Crowdsource
Every human-verified scan permanently reduces the cost of future scans for everyone
What it is
14 verified food products now. pHash fingerprint cache (9 images). Admin /correct endpoint for human verification. Every verified scan: pHash stored → future identical scans return in <10ms for free, no LLM token cost.
The compounding math
At 14 products: marginal impact zero. At 500: LLM calls drop 40%. At 5,000: 80%+ of Indian urban packaged food is covered. At 50,000: Eatlytic's marginal cost per scan approaches ₹0. No competitor can buy this DB — they'd need to do the scans.
Fix needed NOW
pHash Hamming fallback is O(n) not O(1) (BUG-06). Fix this before the cache grows past 1,000 entries or cold-start times spike. Use the BK-tree already in the Blueprint's planned app/ai/perception/ directory.
